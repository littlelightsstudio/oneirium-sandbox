◆ Oneirium — How to Play ◆

What is Oneirium?

Oneirium is a 1v1 strategic card game of dream construction, emotional resonance, and narrative disruption. You are not fighting your opponent. You are building something — while they try to unravel it.

This is what makes Oneirium different from other card games:

Construction, not combat
Your primary goal is creative and generative. You win by completing a coherent, emotionally resonant dream arc — not by reducing an opponent to zero. Attack is a means, not the game.
Emotional arc as scoring engine
No other major card game scores you for meaning. Oneirium scores the emotional coherence of a narrative you construct in real time. Fear becoming Courage. Grief becoming Release. The journey matters.
Tags as emotional vocabulary
Rather than mechanical keywords, Oneirium uses emotional and thematic tags. "I need a Stillness tag here" is a different cognitive experience than any other card game. The vocabulary is human.
Disruption is narrative
When you play a Disturbance into an opponent's dream, you are not reducing their health. You are injecting something dark into their arc. They must respond — or suffer the consequences.
Losing tells a story
When a dream collapses, it doesn't feel like a health bar hitting zero. The Coherence Track drains. The zones shift. The dream unravels. It feels like a nightmare taking over.

The three things you manage

Dream Points (DP)
Your deck-building budget. Every card has a DP cost. Deck total cannot exceed 100 DP. Disturbances have negative DP costs — they refund budget at the cost of instability.
Coherence Points (CP)
The scoring currency. Earned through card values, tag synergies, emotional transitions, and endgame bonuses. Counted only at game end. Highest CP wins.
Coherence Track (CT)
A survival resource, entirely separate from scoring. Starts at 15. Tracks dream stability. Drains when Disturbances land. Collapse at zero means immediate loss — regardless of CP. Managing both systems simultaneously is the skill ceiling of the game.

What drives everything

Tags. Every card carries 1–2 emotional or thematic tags. Matching tags generate synergy. Evolving tags — following a narrative from Fear to Courage, or Grief to Release — generate transitions. Tags are the engine of every scoring layer, every discipline, every strategic decision.

Your board

Each player has one board: a single left-to-right row of cards representing their dream in progress. There are no branches, no parallel rows, no fixed zones. You build one continuous narrative. Maximum 12 cards per row.

Cards are placed freely along the row. New cards extend it at either end, or are inserted between existing cards. Physical adjacency determines all connections — which cards are next to which is the core spatial puzzle of the game.

What adjacency means
Two cards are connected if they sit directly next to each other in the row. Adjacency affects synergy bonuses, certain card actions, and — most importantly — whether a card qualifies as a bridge for a transition.
Transitions need a bridge
A transition completes when a From tag card and a To tag card are both on your arc with at least one card between them. That middle card is the bridge. It can be anything — any type, any tags. What it is doesn't matter. Only that it exists between the two endpoints.
Emotion Slot attachment
Certain cards carry an Emotion Slot (marked with a pink pip on the type line). You may attach one Emotion card to that slot by sliding it under the host. The attached Emotion's tags count from the host's position. It does not take up its own row position.

Typical game length

15–20 total turns. Boards fill to 8–12 cards. When both players complete their arc, scoring begins.

Keeping a dream alive

Every dream has two states happening simultaneously. It is being built — card by card, transition by transition, toward a final score. And it is being tested. Disturbances land in your arc and drain your Coherence Track. You must build something worth scoring while keeping the dream coherent enough to survive. This is the central tension of every game.

CP and CT are completely separate systems. A dream can be scoring magnificently at 24 CP and collapse at zero CT. A dream can be stable at 15 CT and scoring nothing. The skill is managing both at once.

How you score (5 layers)

Layer 1 — Base card values (1–3 CP each)
Every card scores its printed CP regardless of anything else. A 10-year-old can understand this immediately.
Layer 2 — Tag synergy (+1 CP per matching pair)
Two cards sharing a tag = +1 CP per pair. Three cards all carrying Stillness = three pairs = +3 CP. Stackable across the full board.
Layer 3 — Emotional transitions (+3 CP each)
From tag + To tag on arc, at least one card between them. See Transitions tab for all 12 pairs.
Layer 4 — Full Arc Bonus (+5 CP, once)
Three or more completed transitions at game end. The masterwork dream bonus. Rare and deeply satisfying.
Layer 5 — Discipline endgame bonus
Each discipline has one specific endgame condition. See Disciplines tab.

Penalties and bonuses

  • Unresolved Disturbance at game end: −2 CP each
  • Resilience Bonus (CT fell to Critical then recovered to Stable): +3 CP
  • Clean Track Bonus (CT at 15 at game end): +2 CP

Coherence Track zones

Stable — 9 to 15
Normal play. No modifiers.
Unstable — 5 to 8
Maximum 1 card played per turn. Opponent gains +1 CP when placing Disturbances against you.
Critical — 1 to 4
Unstable rules apply. Recover to Stable before game end for +3 CP Resilience Bonus.
Collapse — 0
Immediate loss. The dream unravels into nightmare.

Typical score ranges

  • Chaotic or heavily disrupted: 10–13 CP
  • Solid play, one transition: 16–19 CP
  • Strong play, two transitions: 20–22 CP
  • Expert play, full arc bonus: 23–26 CP
  • Masterwork (full arc + clean track + resilience): 26+ CP

How a transition completes

A transition is a narrative arc expressed through card positioning. When a card with the From tag and a card with the To tag are both on your arc with at least one card physically between them, the transition completes for +3 CP. The Echo effect triggers immediately.

The bridging card can be anything — any type, any tags. It represents the narrative journey between emotional states. Its own tags still contribute independently to synergy and can anchor their own transitions. Nothing on the board is ever wasted.

The single-tag rule

Each tag can only enable one transition at a time. A card carrying two tags can enable two different transitions through its two different tags separately — but one tag cannot anchor two transitions simultaneously. At the moment a transition completes, you declare which tag on each card is serving as the endpoint.

Echo effects

Each transition triggers its Echo effect immediately when it first completes during play — not at game end. The following Echoes are live in the sandbox:

  • Anxiety → Stillness: restores +2 CT immediately
  • Loneliness → Connection: restores +2 CT immediately
  • Grief → Release: opens Disturbance removal picker

Card types

Emotion (1–3 DP)
The emotional spine of any deck. Pure Emotions carry flavor text — their narrative contribution is the world itself. Play standalone in the arc, or tuck under an Emotion Slot card. Choice is permanent.
Character (3–5 DP)
Dream figures. Many carry an Emotion Slot (pink pip on the type line). Notable pairs: Emberling and Emberwing reward each other when both are on the arc.
Setting (3–5 DP)
Environments. Strong tag anchors. True Night adds +2 CP to every synergy pair on the board while present — the most powerful base set Setting.
Situation (2–4 DP)
Narrative turning points. Often have immediate on-play effects. Harmonic Lock protects completed transitions from Disturbances.
Object (2–4 DP)
Flexible synergy connectors. Most provide passive bonuses while on board.
Resolution (3–6 DP)
Clears Disturbances and restores CT. When played, prompts you to choose which Disturbance to remove. CT is restored by that card's drain value.
Disturbance (−1 to −3 DP)
Played into the opponent's arc in a real game. Drains CT immediately on placement. Maximum 2 on any board simultaneously (3 for Dissonants). Refunds DP budget.

Construction rules

  • Exactly 30 cards per deck
  • Maximum 100 DP total budget
  • At least 4 Disturbance cards required (maximum 10)
  • Disturbances refund DP — 6 at −2 DP average = +12 DP back

Recommended composition

Emotions: 8–10 · Characters: 4–6 · Situations: 4–5 · Settings: 3–4 · Objects: 2–3 · Resolutions: 3–4 · Disturbances: 4–10

What goes wrong

  • Too few Emotions: low CP ceiling, transitions hard to build
  • Too many Disturbances: slow board development, hand full of attack cards early
  • Too few Resolutions: fragile CT, vulnerable to pressure

The Disturbance economy

Disturbances have negative DP costs. Running 6 at −2 DP each refunds 12 DP, giving your remaining 24 cards an effective purchasing power of 112 DP. This is the tension: invite chaos, afford power — but flood your hand with attack cards and your own arc suffers.

Mulligan (Draw Mode only)

After building a deck in Draw Mode, a Mulligan button appears. Tap 1 or 2 hand cards to mark them for exchange, then confirm. Those cards go to the bottom of the draw pile and you draw replacements from the top. One mulligan per deck build.

What this sandbox is

This is a single-player sandbox for testing and exploring Oneirium's card set, scoring mechanics, and deck ideas. It is not yet a full game. There is no opponent, no turn structure enforced, and no win condition beyond building the highest-scoring, most stable arc you can.

The sandbox is the right place to discover how transitions chain together, feel how CT pressure changes your decisions, test the Evokers expansion against the base set, and explore the scoring depth before you play against another person.

Sandbox Mode

All deck cards are freely available. No hand limit, no draw pile. Tap any deck card to place it in the arc immediately. Best for exploring card combinations and scoring layers without constraint.

Draw Mode

Simulates the real game hand mechanics. Build Deck shuffles your cards and deals an opening hand of 7.

  • Tap a hand card once to place it in the arc
  • Draw Card pulls the next card from the pile
  • Removing an arc card returns it to your hand
  • On-play draw effects (Dream Drift, Dream Spore, Mudscribe) trigger automatically
  • Mulligan: exchange 1–2 cards before your first placement
