◆ Oneirium — How to Play ◆

What is Oneirium?

A 1v1 strategic card game of dream construction, emotional resonance, and narrative disruption. You are not fighting your opponent. You are building a dream while they try to unravel it.

You win by scoring more Coherence Points (CP) than your opponent, or by forcing their dream to collapse entirely.

Core Concepts

Dream Points (DP)
Deck-building budget. Every card has a DP cost. Deck total cannot exceed 100 DP. Disturbances have negative DP costs.
Coherence Points (CP)
The scoring currency. Counted only at game end. Highest CP wins.
Coherence Track (CT)
Survival resource, not scoring. Starts at 15. Drains when Disturbances land. Collapse at zero = immediate loss.
Tags
Every card carries 1–2 tags. Matching tags generate synergy. Evolving tags generate transitions. Tags are the engine of everything.

Five Scoring Layers

Layer 1 — Base Card Values (1–3 CP each)
Every card scores its printed CP regardless of other conditions.
Layer 2 — Tag Synergy (+1 CP per matching tag pair)
Two cards sharing a tag = +1 CP per pair. Stackable across the board.
Layer 3 — Emotional Transitions (+3 CP each)
From tag card + To tag card on arc, with at least one card between them.
Layer 4 — Full Arc Bonus (+5 CP, once)
Three or more completed transitions at game end.
Layer 5 — Discipline Endgame Bonus
Each discipline has one specific endgame condition.

Penalties & Bonuses

  • Each unresolved Disturbance at game end: −2 CP
  • Resilience Bonus (CT fell to Critical then recovered to Stable): +3 CP
  • Clean Track Bonus (CT at 15 at game end): +2 CP

How Transitions Work

A transition completes when a card with the From tag and a card with the To tag are both on your arc with at least one card between them. That middle card can be anything.

Each tag can only serve one transition at a time. A card with two tags can enable two different transitions through its two different tags, but one tag cannot anchor two transitions simultaneously.

The Coherence Track

Starts at 15. Drains immediately when a Disturbance is placed. Removing a Disturbance restores the CT by its drain value.

Stable — 9 to 15
Normal play. No modifiers.
Unstable — 5 to 8
Max 1 card per turn. Opponent gains +1 CP when placing Disturbances against you.
Critical — 1 to 4
Unstable rules apply. Recover to Stable before game end for +3 CP Resilience Bonus.
Collapse — 0
Immediate loss. The dream unravels.

Types & Category Colors

Emotion (1–3 DP)
Emotional tone. Played standalone in the arc row, or tucked under an Emotion Slot card. Choice is permanent. Does not occupy a row position when tucked.
Character (3–5 DP)
Dream figures. Many carry an Emotion Slot (pink circle pip on the card).
Setting (3–5 DP)
Environments and locations. Strong tag anchors.
Situation (2–4 DP)
Narrative events. Often have immediate actions on play.
Object (2–4 DP)
Symbolic or functional items. Flexible synergy connectors.
Resolution (3–6 DP)
Clears Disturbances and can restore CT.
Disturbance (−1 to −3 DP)
Played into the opponent's arc. Drains CT immediately on placement. Max 2 on any board simultaneously.

Building Your Deck

  • Exactly 30 cards, maximum 100 DP total
  • At least 4 Disturbance cards required (maximum 10)
  • Disturbances refund DP budget, letting you afford stronger cards

Recommended Composition

Emotions: 8–10 · Characters: 4–6 · Situations: 4–5 · Settings: 3–4 · Objects: 2–3 · Resolutions: 3–4 · Disturbances: 4–10
  • Too few Emotions: low CP ceiling
  • Too many Disturbances: slow board development
  • Too few Resolutions: fragile CT

What works right now

Full 60-card base set
All 60 cards browsable and playable. Category filters and colour-coded backgrounds by card type.
Deck building
Build Deck randomly assembles a legal deck within 100 DP. Manual card addition also supported.
Live scoring
All five scoring layers calculate in real time: base CP, tag synergy, transitions (+3 CP each), Full Arc Bonus (+5), Disturbance penalties. Each tag can only serve one transition at a time.
Coherence Track
Starts at 15. Drains on Disturbance placement. Zone indicators update in real time. Collapse at zero. Disturbance tags contribute to transitions when on your arc.

Not yet built

  • Opponent board and two-player turn structure
  • Card actions triggering automatically
  • Resolution card mechanics (removing Disturbances from arc)
  • Discipline passives and endgame bonuses
  • Hand management and draw mechanics

What comes next

Opponent board and basic two-player turn structure. Then Disturbance placement on the opponent's arc. Then the full MVP in Claude Code as a proper multi-file project.
