What is Oneirium?

Oneirium is a 1v1 strategic card game of dream construction, emotional resonance, and narrative disruption. You are not fighting your opponent. You are building a dream while they try to unravel it.

You win by scoring more Coherence Points (CP) than your opponent by building a stable, emotionally resonant dream arc. Or by forcing your opponent's dream to collapse entirely.

Core Concepts

Dream Points (DP)
Your deck-building budget. Every card has a printed DP cost. Your deck total cannot exceed 100 DP. Disturbance cards have negative DP costs, refunding budget at the risk of instability.
Coherence Points (CP)
The scoring currency. Earned through base card values, tag synergies, emotional transitions, and endgame bonuses. Counted only at game end. Highest CP wins.
Coherence Track (CT)
A survival resource, not a scoring resource. Starts at 15. Tracks dream stability. Degrades when Disturbances land on your board. Collapse at zero means immediate loss.
Tags
Every card carries 1-2 tags: emotional, thematic, or symbolic descriptors. Tags are the engine of everything. Matching tags generate synergy. Evolving tags generate transitions.

Five Scoring Layers

All scoring is calculated at game end. Layers apply in order.

Layer 1 — Base Card Values (1–3 CP each)
Every card scores its printed CP value regardless of any other conditions. A 10-year-old can understand this immediately.
Layer 2 — Tag Synergy (+1 CP per matching tag pair)
Any two cards on your board sharing a tag score +1 CP per pair. Stackable across the full board. Three cards all carrying Stillness = three pairs = +3 CP.
Layer 3 — Emotional Transitions (+3 CP each)
When a card with a From tag and a card with a To tag (from the Transition Pair List) are both on your board with at least one card between them, the transition completes for +3 CP.
Layer 4 — Full Arc Bonus (+5 CP, once)
Three or more completed transitions on your board at game end. The masterwork dream bonus. Rare and deeply satisfying.
Layer 5 — Discipline Endgame Bonus
Each discipline has one specific endgame condition scoring additional CP.

Penalties & Bonuses

  • Each unresolved Disturbance at game end: −2 CP
  • Resilience Bonus: CT fell to Critical (1–4) then recovered to Stable (9+): +3 CP
  • Clean Track Bonus: CT at 15 when game ends: +2 CP

How Transitions Work

A transition completes when a card with the From tag and a card with the To tag are both on your arc with at least one card between them.

The bridging card can be anything. It represents the narrative journey between emotional states. Its own tags still contribute to synergy independently.

Each tag can only serve one transition at a time. A card with two tags can enable two different transitions using its two tags separately, but one tag cannot anchor two transitions simultaneously.

When a transition completes during play, its Echo effect triggers immediately.

The Coherence Track

The CT is a public survival resource. Both players' tracks are visible at all times. It is entirely separate from CP scoring.

Each Disturbance card has a printed CT drain value, applied immediately on placement. Removing a Disturbance restores the CT by that amount.

CT Zones

Stable — 9 to 15
Normal play. No modifiers.
Unstable — 5 to 8
Maximum 1 card played per turn. Opponent gains +1 CP when placing Disturbances against you.
Critical — 1 to 4
Unstable rules apply. If you recover back to Stable before game end, gain the Resilience Bonus (+3 CP).
Collapse — 0
Immediate loss. The dream unravels into nightmare.

Disturbance Drain Reference

  • Weak Disturbance (−1 DP): −1 CT on placement
  • Standard Disturbance (−2 DP): −2 CT on placement
  • Strong Disturbance (−3 DP): −3 to −4 CT on placement

Card Types

Emotion (1–3 DP)
Emotional tone. Can be played standalone in the arc row, or tucked under a card with an Emotion Slot. When tucked, it belongs entirely to the host card's position and does not take up its own row position. The choice is permanent.
Character (3–5 DP)
Dream figures with traits and synergy potential. Many carry an Emotion Slot (marked with a pink circle pip).
Setting (3–5 DP)
Environments and locations. Provide strong tag anchors and often carry Emotion Slots.
Situation (2–4 DP)
Narrative events and turning points. Often have immediate card actions on play.
Object (2–4 DP)
Symbolic or functional dream items. Flexible synergy connectors.
Resolution (3–6 DP)
Clears Disturbances and can restore CT. Essential for managing your track.
Disturbance (−1 to −3 DP)
Played into the opponent's arc. Drains their CT immediately on placement. Suppresses synergy or breaks transition chains. Max 2 on any board simultaneously (3 for the Dissonants discipline).

What this sandbox is

This is a single-player design and scoring sandbox for Oneirium. Its purpose is to let you explore the card set, test dream arc combinations, and feel how the scoring and CT mechanics work in practice. It is not yet a full game.

What works right now

Full 60-card base set
All 60 base set cards are browsable and playable. Category filters let you navigate by type. Card actions are displayed on each card for reference, though they do not trigger automatically yet.
Deck building
Build Deck randomly assembles a legal deck within 100 DP with a minimum of 4 Disturbances. You can also manually add any card from the base set by tapping it. The DP bar tracks your budget in real time.
Dream Arc placement
Tap any deck card once to place it in the arc. Tap an arc card to remove it. Desktop: drag cards directly. Up to 12 cards per arc.
Live scoring
All five scoring layers calculate in real time as you build: base CP, tag synergy pairs, emotional transitions (+3 CP each), the Full Arc Bonus (+5 CP for three or more transitions), and Disturbance CP penalties. Completed transitions and active synergies are shown as hint badges below the arc.
Coherence Track
The CT starts at 15 and drains immediately when a Disturbance lands in the arc. Zone indicators (Stable, Unstable, Critical, Collapse) update in real time. Removing a Disturbance restores CT. The Resolve button simulates a Resolution card restoring +2 CT. Collapse at zero triggers the dream collapse screen.
Transition detection
All 12 transition pairs are detected automatically. Each tag can only serve one transition at a time. A card with two tags can enable two different transitions through its two tags independently.

What is not yet built

  • Opponent board and two-player mode
  • Turn structure and turn limits
  • Card actions triggering automatically (Emotion Slot attachment, draw effects, CT restoration on play, etc.)
  • Disturbance placement on an opponent's board
  • Resolution card mechanics (removing Disturbances)
  • Discipline passives and endgame bonuses
  • Hand management and draw mechanics
  • Resilience Bonus and Clean Track Bonus tracking

What comes next

The next planned step is opponent board and basic two-player turn structure. Once that is in place, Disturbance placement, Resolution mechanics, and card actions will follow. The full digital MVP will be built in Claude Code as a proper multi-file project.

Building Your Deck

  • Exactly 30 cards per deck
  • Maximum 100 DP total budget
  • At least 4 Disturbance cards required (maximum 10)
  • Disturbances refund DP, letting you afford stronger cards elsewhere

Recommended Composition

Emotions: 8–10  ·  Characters: 4–6  ·  Situations: 4–5  ·  Settings: 3–4  ·  Objects: 2–3  ·  Resolutions: 3–4  ·  Disturbances: 4–10

What Happens Without Balance

  • Too few Emotions: low CP ceiling, transitions hard to build
  • Too many Disturbances: slow board development, hand full of attack cards early
  • Too few Resolutions: fragile CT, vulnerable to Disturbance pressure

Budget Math

6 Disturbances at −2 DP average = 12 DP refunded. Effective purchasing power for remaining 24 cards: 112 DP. This is the Disturbance economy: invite chaos, afford power.
